Network

Wiki Jet serves a focused inter-island network across Hawaii, offering pilots short and scenic hops.

Daniel K. Inouye International Airport (PHNL)

Hawaii’s primary aviation gateway and largest metropolitan center.


Longest Runway: 12,360 ft


Fun Fact: Honolulu’s airport has ocean-edge runways, giving planes stunning sea views during takeoff and landing. Normal operations have aircraft departing to the east where they must make quick right turns to avoid terrain.

Lihue Airport (PHLI)

Kauai’s main airport serving resort and inter‑island traffic.


Longest Runway: 6,500 ft


Fun Fact: In a deleted scene of the film Lilo & Stitch; Stitch, Nani, Jumba, and Pleakley hijacked a Boeing 747 jet from this airport, scraping against buildings through downtown Honolulu. After the September 11 attacks, with only a few weeks left in production, the climax was completely reworked to have them use Jumba's spacecraft, and through the Kauaʻi mountains.


Ellison Onizuka Kona International Airport at Keāhole (PHKO)

Kona serves the leeward (west) portion of the big island.


Longest Runway: 11,000 ft


Fun Fact: On January 8, 2017, the airport was renamed Ellison Onizuka Kona International Airport at Keāhole to honor astronaut Ellison Onizuka who was born and raised in Kona and died in the Space Shuttle Challenger disaster.


Hilo International Airport (PHTO)

Hilo serves the windward (east) portion of the big island.


Longest Runway: 9,800 ft


Fun Fact: On April 28, 1988, Aloha Airlines Flight 243 from Hilo to Honolulu was carrying 89 passengers and 5 crew members when the plane experienced rapid decompression from a section of the fuselage tearing away from the airplane.


Kahului Airport (PHOG)

The main airport servicing Maui.


Longest Runway: 6,998 ft


Fun Fact: In response to the Attack on Pearl Harbor on nearby Oahu, the U.S. entered World War II. The demands of war required a proper air station on Maui, so the Navy acquired approximately 1,341 acres (543 ha) of sugar cane fields near Kahului and beginning construction of the airfield in 1942.
